## **EVENT SUMMARY**

Following the onset of Z-314-S’s persistent split-state condition, Operator Linda granted the subject access to her personal quarters for observation and controlled interaction.

Operator Linda’s quarters are designated as enhanced-grade housing due to her recognition as **“Operator of the Year” (2024–2026)** and contain specialized equipment, including restricted weaponry.

During the visit, Z-314-S exhibited immediate visual fixation on a pair of close-combat kunai stored within the room. These weapons are designed for short-range ambush and lethality, not for throwing.

---

## **INCIDENT DESCRIPTION**

Operator Linda verbally identified the weapons and described their effectiveness. During this explanation, she issued a delayed warning regarding their coating.

Z-314-S made direct contact with the blade, resulting in a minor laceration to her finger.

The warning was completed immediately after contact:

The kunai were coated with a high-potency neurotoxin classified among the most lethal synthetic compounds in Aperture records.

Within seconds of exposure:

* Subdermal veins surrounding the wound darkened visibly  
* Discoloration spread rapidly across the affected area  
* Subject lost consciousness and collapsed

Emergency response protocols were initiated immediately. Z-314-S was transported to the ER Wing.

---

## **MEDICAL ASSESSMENT**

Attending physician: **Dr. █████████**

Key findings:

* The neurotoxin does **not function as intended on Starladian biology**  
* Full systemic shutdown did not occur  
* However, the toxin remained **biologically active within the bloodstream**

Conclusion:

“The toxin doesn’t work on her the way it should, but it hasn’t been neutralized. It’s still present, still active—we have to wait for it to wear off, if it does.”

Subject remained unconscious for approximately **5 hours**.

---

## **POST-RECOVERY CONDITION**

Z-314-S regained consciousness with no immediate external distress and delivered a single statement:

“I feel funny.”

Subsequent analysis revealed a critical anomalous development.

---

## **ANOMALOUS EFFECT — TOXIN SATURATION**

The neurotoxin has fully integrated into Z-314-S’s internal biological systems.

As a result:

* Blood composition is now **toxin-saturated**  
* Previously documented **healing properties are no longer present**  
* All tested biological outputs now carry toxic properties

Confirmed affected substances:

* Blood  
* Tears  
* Open wound discharge  
* Residual fluids from claw strikes or physical contact

The toxin remains stable within the subject and does not degrade under normal observation periods.

---

## **HAZARD PROFILE**

Z-314-S is now classified as a **biological contact hazard**.

Exposure risks include:

* Rapid neurotoxic reaction upon bloodstream entry  
* Unknown effects from surface-level exposure (ongoing testing)  
* Potential lethality depending on dosage and exposure method

Even minor contact (e.g., scratches, contaminated surfaces) is considered dangerous.

---

## **SAFETY DIRECTIVES**

* Z-314-S’s blood

Additional measures:

* Medical-grade protective equipment required during close proximity  
* Immediate decontamination protocols upon suspected exposure  
* Restricted access to hazardous equipment within operator quarters
