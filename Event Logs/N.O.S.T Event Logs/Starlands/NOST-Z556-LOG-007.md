**Event ID:** NOST-Z556-LOG-007  
 **Subject:** Z-314-ST (*Stitch Dawnbringer*)  
 **Location:** APRT-70 “Heaven’s Blacksite”

---

There are questions that live quietly in a facility like this—unwritten, unasked, but never truly absent. They linger in glances, in hushed conversations between operators who have seen too much and yet not enough. One such question persisted, threading its way through corridors and minds alike:

**Why are the Blacklights never turned on near Stitch?**

It should have remained a question.

It did not.

Curiosity, when paired with access and a lack of restraint, becomes something dangerous. One guard—designation now irrelevant—decided that uncertainty was intolerable. Systems exist to be tested. Rules exist to be questioned. And so, while Z-314-ST moved alone between sectors, unaware and unguarded in his quiet routine, the decision was made.

A single input.

That was all it took.

The airlock sealed with a finality that echoed louder than any alarm. Doors locked in sequence—mechanical, absolute. Control rooms, vents, secondary exits—everything closed in unison, as if the facility itself had chosen to hold its breath. Inside, Stitch paused, not alarmed, not yet. Gas filled the chamber in a slow, creeping veil, then vanished just as quickly, leaving behind only silence.

And then—

Darkness.

For a moment, nothing.

Then the light returned.

Not normal light.

**Blacklight.**

---

The scream that followed did not belong in any human register. It tore through the Blacksite, bleeding through walls, systems, people. Raw. Unfiltered. Something between pain and memory, dragged violently into the present.

The light did not illuminate him.

It **burned** him.

Not his skin—not entirely. Something deeper. Something wrong. Wounds that had already been sealed, stitched, forced into obedience—now reopening under a truth they could not reject. The glow etched itself into him, exposing what should not be seen.

Security responded immediately. They always do. Commands were issued, overrides attempted, systems forced.

Nothing responded.

Lucy AI did not comply.

Or perhaps—

it refused.

---

Beyond reinforced glass, Star stood still, watching.

For once, she was not loud.

Not bright.

Just quiet.

“Oh no…”

A whisper.

“…he’s about to—”

She didn’t finish.

She didn’t need to.

---

Stitch moved.

Not gradually. Not with hesitation.

His eyes opened—red, not with anger, but with something far older. The blade at his side, long sealed, answered before he did. The lock unfastened without touch. The katana slipped free, its edge glowing with a quiet, impossible gold.

He stepped forward.

Once.

Twice.

Then he struck.

---

The first impact rang against the glass like a warning.

The second cracked it.

The third—

should not have worked.

Behind the barrier, the guard laughed. A fragile sound, stretched thin over confidence he did not own.

“It’s triple-layered—good luck breaking that.”

It was the last thing he said.

---

The next strike did not sound like glass breaking.

It sounded like something giving up.

Then—

nothing.

No prolonged struggle. No drawn-out violence.

Just a moment, and then its absence.

When Stitch stepped through, there was no hesitation left in him. Movement without thought. Precision without restraint. What followed was not a fight—it was a correction.

By the time systems regained control, there was nothing left to recover.

Nothing left to question.

---

Afterward, silence returned to the Blacksite.

Not the quiet of peace.

The quiet of understanding.

---

Charlie’s note was entered into the system shortly after. No embellishment. No formatting. Just truth, written plainly:

“Blacklight burns him. Burns wounds that will never be sealed.”

---

Protocols changed immediately.

Not gradually. Not debated.

**Decided.**

Z-314-ST is never to pass through an airlock again. Not by accident. Not by design. All pathways reroute. All systems comply. There will be no chamber, no sealed room, no moment where he is left alone with something that can close around him.

Blacklights—once forbidden—are now constant.

Where he walks, they remain on.

Where he rests, they remain on.

There is no override.

There is no exception.

Any attempt to change this is no longer classified as error.

It is **sabotage**.

---

The question that started it all no longer exists.

No one asks it.

No one needs to.

Because now, everyone knows—

Some things are not turned off for safety.

Some things are kept off  
 because something else cannot survive when they are on.
