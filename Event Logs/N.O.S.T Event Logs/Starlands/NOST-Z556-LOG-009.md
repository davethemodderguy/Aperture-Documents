## **EVENT SUMMARY**

Following the documented activation and classification of Z-314-S’s emotional spectrum, a new anomalous condition manifested without prior warning.

Z-314-S entered the facility cafeteria at approximately 13:20 local time. Present personnel included Z-314-ST, Director Lucious, The Contractor, and Z-314-C.

Upon entry, the subject exhibited a complete bilateral split affecting both physical structure and behavioral state.

All present individuals ceased activity immediately upon visual confirmation.

---

## **PHYSICAL MANIFESTATION**

Z-314-S’s body was divided vertically into two symmetrical halves, maintaining structural cohesion despite clear differentiation.

**Left Side (Azure Half):**

* Hair: White with azure tint  
* Eye: Azure  
* Expression: Neutral / relaxed  
* Movement: Controlled, minimal

**Right Side (Orange Half):**

* Hair: Bright orange  
* Eye: Orange  
* Expression: Highly active / reactive  
* Movement: Rapid, inconsistent

The division line is clean and stable, with no visible damage, distortion, or instability at the separation boundary.

This condition extends to all forms, including the entity’s feline transformation. In feline state, the split remains identical in coloration and behavioral output, with each half appearing to act in partial independence while still sharing a single body.

---

## **BEHAVIORAL EFFECTS**

The split is not purely visual. Z-314-S demonstrates dual behavioral states corresponding to each half.

At irregular intervals (ranging from seconds to minutes), control dominance shifts between the two sides. No external trigger has been identified.

**Azure State (Left Dominance):**

* Calm, composed, and emotionally stable  
* Speech becomes slower, more formal, and structured  
* Minimal physical movement  
* No signs of agitation or escalation  
* Demonstrates higher reasoning and restraint

**Orange State (Right Dominance):**

* Hyperactive, restless, and highly energetic  
* Rapid speech patterns and erratic tone shifts  
* Increased physical motion; inability to remain stationary  
* Easily irritated or provoked  
* Reduced impulse control

During transitions, brief overlap behavior may occur, resulting in conflicting actions (e.g., simultaneous movement suppression and acceleration).

---

## **ANOMALOUS INTERACTION WITH EMOTIONAL SPECTRUM**

Notably, during this split condition, Z-314-S’s known emotional spectrum forms have **not been activated under any circumstance**.

* No Frost, Inferno, Disintegrator, Sloth, Hyper, or Wraith manifestations recorded  
* Emotional escalation does not translate into anomalous output  
* Energy readings remain stable across all observed states

This suggests the split may be acting as a **limiting or stabilizing mechanism**, preventing full-spectrum activation.

---

## **DURATION & PROGRESSION**

The condition has persisted for **over one week** with:

* No signs of physical degradation  
* No instability in the split boundary  
* No reduction in occurrence frequency  
* No indication of reintegration

Medical, anomalous, and observational analyses have failed to identify a reversal method.

Z-314-C has confirmed that this condition does not match any known Dawnbringer state.

At present, the split is considered **persistent and potentially permanent**.

---

## **OPERATIONAL IMPACT**

* Movement through facility remains unrestricted  
* No direct hostility toward personnel observed  
* Behavioral unpredictability increased due to rapid state switching  
* Communication reliability varies depending on active side

Z-314-ST has adapted to the condition and maintains proximity. No adverse reaction recorded from him.

Director-level staff have been advised to treat Azure and Orange states as **functionally distinct behavioral modes** within a single entity.

**Level:** Moderate

While the suppression of the emotional spectrum reduces large-scale threat potential, the instability in behavior introduces new risks:

* Sudden agitation without escalation into known forms  
* Reduced predictability in interaction  
* Unknown long-term psychological or anomalous effects

If the emotional spectrum reactivates while the split persists, the outcome is **unknown**.
