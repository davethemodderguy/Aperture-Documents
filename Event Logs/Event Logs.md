# Event Logs

**APERTURE INDUSTRIES**  
 **EVENT LOG SYSTEM — STRUCTURAL OVERVIEW**  
 **Classification: Internal**

---

### **I. PURPOSE**

**Event Logs** are the official record of incidents occurring within Aperture Industries.  
 They exist to document, preserve, and analyze events that impact operations, personnel, or assets.

Unlike static records, Event Logs capture **change** — moments where something goes wrong, right, or beyond expectation.

---

### **II. GENERAL FUNCTION**

All Event Logs serve three core functions:

* **Documentation** — Record exactly what occurred  
* **Accountability** — Identify causes, responses, and responsible parties  
* **Analysis** — Provide data for future prevention or replication

They are written with precision and minimal interpretation.  
 Speculation is restricted unless clearly marked.

---

### **III. CLASSIFICATION BY DIVISION**

Due to the scale and diversity of Aperture operations, Event Logs are divided into three categories:

---

#### **1\. GENERAL EVENT LOGS**

**Scope:**  
 Facility-wide or administrative incidents not tied to specialized divisions.

**Examples Include:**

* Structural failures  
* Power outages  
* Internal conflicts or security alerts  
* System malfunctions  
* Non-anomalous emergencies

**Purpose:**  
 To maintain operational continuity and ensure that foundational systems remain stable.

These logs answer a simple question:  
 **“Did the facility itself fail?”**

---

#### **2\. C.O.R.E EVENT LOGS**

*(Central Operations & Research Executive)*

**Scope:**  
 Incidents involving:

* Entity containment  
* Experimental procedures  
* Weapon systems  
* Blacksite operations

**Examples Include:**

* Containment breaches  
* Failed experiments  
* Weapon malfunctions  
* Neutralization operations

**Purpose:**  
 To track and refine Aperture’s control over anomalies.

These logs answer:  
 **“Did we lose control of what we were handling?”**

---

#### **3\. N.O.S.T EVENT LOGS**

*(Non-Ordinary Space & Territory Division)*

**Scope:**  
 Incidents beyond Earth or involving non-terrestrial phenomena.

**Examples Include:**

* Interdimensional breaches  
* Extraterrestrial encounters  
* Portal instability  
* Cosmic-scale anomalies

**Purpose:**  
 To document the unknown and maintain survivability beyond conventional reality.

These logs answer:  
 **“Did something beyond our world interfere—or respond?”**

---

### **IV. WHY EVENT LOGS ARE SEPARATED**

A unified logging system would introduce critical failures in clarity and response.

Each division operates under fundamentally different conditions:

* **GENERAL** deals with infrastructure and human systems  
* **C.O.R.E** deals with contained threats  
* **N.O.S.T** deals with the uncontrollable unknown

Combining them would:

* Blur responsibility  
* Delay response time  
* Compromise data accuracy

Separation ensures:

* **Clear chains of command**  
* **Faster analysis and reaction**  
* **Accurate historical records per domain**

---

### **V. STRUCTURAL SIGNIFICANCE**

Event Logs are not optional records.  
 They are **post-incident truth**.

Every failure, breach, or anomaly leaves behind data.  
 That data determines whether the next incident is contained—

—or repeated.

---

### **VI. FINAL NOTE**

If Entity Documents define **what exists**,  
 and Protocols define **what to do**,

**Event Logs define what actually happened.**
