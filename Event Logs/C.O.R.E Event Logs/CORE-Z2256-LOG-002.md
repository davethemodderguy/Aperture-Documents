### **Event ID:** CORE-Z2256-LOG-002

**Subject:** Z-2256 (“Candleheart”)  
**Related Entities:** Z-126 (“Endministrator”), Star, Bao  
**Classification:** Internal / Psychological-Anomalous Incident

---

### **SUMMARY**

Approximately **5 weeks after the manifestation of Z-126**, Z-2256 began exhibiting **severe behavioral and physiological deviations** consistent with an anomalous emotional collapse event, later classified as **“Heartbreak State.”**

This marks the **first recorded instability case** for Z-2256.

---

### **OBSERVED CHANGES**

Personnel reports and surveillance confirm the following progressive alterations:

- **Magenta glow fading → desaturated gray**
- **Cranial flame extinguished completely**
- Significant **drop in activity and helpful behavior**
- Prolonged periods of **passive floating**, both inside and outside the Blacksite
- **Pendant (core emotional marker)** visibly altered from:
    - _Heart (intact)_ → _Heart (fractured)_

Behavioral shifts:

- Social withdrawal
- Reduced verbal communication
- Avoidance of key entities (Z-126, Bao)
- Resistance to interaction

---

### **INITIAL RESPONSE**

Aperture personnel initiated **mass emotional support attempts**, including:

- Direct questioning
- Physical comfort attempts
- Continuous presence

**Result:** Negative

Z-2256 displayed:

- Irritation toward attention
- Accelerated visual degradation
- Increased emotional instability

**Conclusion:**  
Excessive, unfocused emotional engagement **worsens Heartbreak State**

---

### **ESCALATION**

Z-2256 began **full social isolation**, including distancing from:

- Z-126 (“Endministrator”)
- Bao
- General personnel

At this stage, **visual darkening intensified exponentially**, indicating a potential approach toward critical instability.

---

### **INTERVENTION — STAR**

Operator **Star** initiated alternative approach:

- No questioning
- No forced interaction
- Passive presence only

After prolonged exposure, Z-2256 voluntarily disclosed the cause of distress.

---

### **CAUSE OF HEARTBREAK**

Z-2256 reported the following incident:

- Assigned to observe **Aperture Mortality Preservation System (A.M.P.S)** maintenance
- Awaiting technician arrival
- Technician suffered accidental fall through ventilation system
- Z-2256 attempted interception **but failed**

**Result:** Technician fatality

Z-2256 internalized the event as **personal failure**, triggering emotional collapse.

---

### **RESOLUTION EVENT**

Upon hearing the account, Operator Star responded with:

> _Laughter_

Star then retrieved the deceased technician and demonstrated **post-mortem recovery** (standard under A.M.P.S-supported conditions).

Statement issued:

> _“Death don't work around here, unless you really deserve it.”_

---

### **OUTCOME**

Immediate reversal observed:

- Magenta coloration restored
- Cranial flame reignited
- Pendant returned to **intact heart state**
- Behavioral normalization resumed

Z-2256 returned to:

- Active assistance roles
- Positive emotional baseline
- Social reintegration

---

### **ANALYSIS**

Key findings:

- Z-2256 possesses **extreme emotional sensitivity tied directly to physical state**
- Misinterpretation of events can trigger **critical instability**
- Traditional emotional support methods are **ineffective or harmful**
- **Targeted, minimal, trust-based interaction** is the most effective stabilization method

---

### **UPDATED PROTOCOL**

In the event of future Heartbreak State:

- **DO NOT** crowd or overwhelm Z-2256
- **DO NOT** initiate mass comfort attempts
- Assign **single trusted individual** (Priority: Star)
- Allow subject to **voluntarily communicate**
- Clarify misunderstandings **factually, not emotionally**
