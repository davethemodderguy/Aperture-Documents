I stopped her today.

It didn’t feel real at first, the kind of moment your mind tries to soften before it understands what it’s looking at. Star was standing there, still as ever, but not in the quiet way she’s been these past days—this was different, heavier, like the air itself didn’t want to move. In her hand was a knife. Not just any blade, but the one Stitch forged for her, the one meant for something gentle, something simple, something like tending to her garden. It was never meant to be held like this.

She held it close.

Too close.

Her eyes gave her away, even beneath the shadow. There was something in them, something final, something that didn’t look like hesitation at first, but the longer I watched, the more I realized it was the only thing keeping everything from ending right there. The blade was right next to her neck, so close it barely needed to move. One motion. One cut. That was all it would take.

And for a moment… I thought she would.

But she didn’t.

Something stopped her, something small but unbreakable in that exact second, and I didn’t understand it until I followed her gaze. Her eyes weren’t lost, weren’t empty. They were focused—locked onto something outside the room. I turned, slowly, and that’s when I realized where she was looking.

At me.

Through the window.

I don’t know how long I had been standing there, or how long she had known. But she saw me. She was watching me the entire time, even when the blade was at her throat, even when everything was about to fall apart. And in that moment, something shifted—not around her, not in the room, but in understanding.

The reason she didn’t let go.

The reason she’s still here.

The reason she keeps holding on, even when everything in her is breaking.

It’s because of me.

The knife slipped slightly in her hand, just enough for the moment to break, and I moved without thinking. I forced the door open—frost resisting, metal cracking under the strain—and closed the distance before she could lift it again. I grabbed her wrist, harder than I meant to, colder than I expected, like holding onto something that wasn’t entirely solid anymore.

She resisted.

Not violently at first, just enough to pull back, like she didn’t want to hurt me but didn’t want to let go either. The blade shifted between us, unstable, caught in a moment where neither of us had full control. I tried to take it from her, tried to pull it away, but her grip tightened, instinct or something deeper refusing to release it.

Then it turned into something else.

Not a fight in the way people think of one, not anger or aggression, but a desperate, silent struggle between holding on and letting go. The knife moved again, too fast, too close—

—and I felt it.

A sharp, sudden break in the moment.

Pain.

Not deep, not fatal, but enough.

Enough to make her stop.

The blade slipped from her hand, falling against the frozen floor with a sound that felt louder than it should have been. Her eyes changed then, not fully, not completely, but enough for me to see it—that flicker of something still alive, something still there beneath everything else.

She looked at me.

At the injury.

And for the first time in days… she reacted.

Not with words, not with a smile, but with something closer to fear.

I didn’t care about the wound. I barely felt it anymore. All I could think about was the fact that she stopped. That in that moment, between ending everything and hurting me, she chose neither. She let go.

I told her it was okay.

I don’t know if it was a lie or not.

I told her she didn’t have to do this, that she didn’t have to carry everything alone, that she could stay, just stay, even if it hurt, even if it didn’t get better right away. My voice didn’t sound steady, but I said it anyway, because it was all I had.

She didn’t answer.

But she didn’t reach for the knife again.

She just stood there, closer than before, quieter than before, like something inside her had been interrupted at the last possible moment. The frost around us didn’t disappear, didn’t weaken, but it stopped getting worse.

And that was enough.

For now.

Because today… she almost let go.

And the only thing that stopped her—

was me.
