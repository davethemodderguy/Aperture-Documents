02/08/2025

I approached Star today.

The mist came and went in quiet pulses, falling in short, uncertain breaths, like even the sky couldn’t decide whether to stay or disappear. It clung to the ground, to the walls, to everything it touched, softening edges, blurring distance, making the world feel smaller than it was. And somewhere within that shifting haze… she stood.

But what I saw—

was not something I recognized.

Not in the way her other forms made sense, not in the way Frost became Grief, not in the way pain could be understood if you stayed with it long enough. This… felt like something beyond that. Something that forms after the breaking, after the silence, after the moment where everything should have ended but didn’t.

She wore a pure white dress.

Not untouched, not innocent, but empty in a way that made it feel heavier than anything she had worn before. It didn’t carry warmth, didn’t carry life—it simply existed, draped over her like something that had forgotten what it was meant to represent. And on her back… a knife.

Embedded.

Still.

But not real.

Not in the way it could be removed, not in the way it could bleed her again. It was something else—something that remained because it *needed* to be seen. A mark that refused to fade. A truth that refused to be hidden.

Betrayal, made visible.

Her eyes were crying.

But even that… was wrong.

The tears didn’t fall like water. They didn’t soften her face, didn’t blur into something human or gentle. They fell like fragments—cold, hardened, like pieces of something that had already broken long before they reached the surface. And when they touched the ground, they didn’t disappear. They lingered, stubborn, unmoving, like even her sorrow refused to leave.

Her halo…

was shattered.

Not dimmed, not cracked, not weakened.

Shattered.

Like something sacred had been taken apart without care, without hesitation, without regret.

And one of her wings—

was gone.

Not hidden.

Not folded.

Gone.

Torn away in a way that left nothing to mistake, nothing to reinterpret, nothing to soften the truth of what had been done to her.

And when she saw me…

she cried more.

Not louder, not violently, not in a way that asked to be heard—but deeper, like something inside her had been waiting for someone to witness it, like the moment she realized she wasn’t alone, everything she had been holding back began to fall apart all at once.

She used what little strength she had left.

And she spoke.

“It’s Betrayal.”

Just two words.

And they were enough to break something in me.

Because I remembered.

Not clearly, not all at once, but in fragments that forced themselves back together the moment she said it. The first time I saw her like that—the wing already gone, her halo already broken, her body too still against the ground. The blood… too much of it, pooling beneath her, spreading in a way that didn’t belong to something that was supposed to live.

She was too young to lose that much.

Too fragile to survive it without consequence.

And yet—

she was smiling.

Even then.

Even in that moment, with everything taken from her, with her body failing and her light breaking, she still smiled.

Star wasn’t just attacked.

She was betrayed.

Not by something distant. Not by something unknown.

By someone close enough to reach her.

Someone she didn’t guard against.

Someone she trusted.

She gave everything to everyone. Not selectively, not carefully, but completely. She didn’t measure what she gave, didn’t hold anything back for herself. She made this place feel like something it was never meant to be—a home, something warm, something human.

And someone answered that—

with a blade.

Not just to her body.

But to everything she believed in.

And even after that…

she stayed.

She continues to give, continues to help, continues to move through the facility like nothing happened, like if she keeps going long enough, the past will lose its weight, like she can bury it beneath everything she does for others.

Like she can outrun it.

I told her the investigation is ongoing.

That we’re looking.

That whoever did this hasn’t been ignored, hasn’t been erased.

But the words felt thin.

Because she already knew more than we did.

She told me what happened.

Not in detail, not in a way that filled every gap, but enough.

It wasn’t a fight.

It wasn’t something she saw coming.

She was backstabbed.

Her vision went cloudy the moment it happened, her body failing faster than her mind could process, the world slipping away before she could even understand what she was feeling. But even then… even in that fading moment…

she saw them.

She saw the one who did it.

They aimed for her head.

A second strike.

A final one.

Something that would have ended everything.

But they hesitated.

Just for a moment.

And that moment…

is why she’s still here.

She told me something else.

Something I can’t ignore.

They didn’t want to do it.

Something forced them.

We checked the systems.

Three calls.

All connected to Urbanshade.

Incoming.

Outgoing.

Precise.

Hidden.

Whoever made them… erased themselves too well. No identity. No trace strong enough to follow. Just the knowledge that something moved through our systems without leaving a face behind.

And the cameras—

the only thing that could have shown the truth—

were frozen.

Encased in the frost from her Queen of Frost form, locked in something that refused to melt, something no one could approach without risk, something that preserved the moment by hiding it completely.

So what happened in that room…

is gone.

Or worse—

it’s being kept from us.

I don’t understand why Urbanshade would do this.

Not to her.

Not when Charlie would react the way she did. Not when Stitch—when he saw her—wouldn’t even need words to understand what was taken. Not when Director Lucious exists. Not when The Contractor would never allow something like this to pass without consequence.

Not even Lucy AI would stay silent.

So why.

Why her.

And then…

I realized something I wish I didn’t.

Morale dropped after her attack.

Not slowly.

Not naturally.

But sharply.

Especially among us Operators.

And that kind of damage…

feels intentional.

Like it was meant to break more than just her.

Like she wasn’t the only target.

So now I’m left standing here, in a place that feels quieter than it should, heavier than it was, holding onto something that doesn’t feel like a question anymore—

but the beginning of something much worse.

What were they thinking?

And more importantly—

How far down does this go?
