Star is in her Frost form.

If you don’t know what that is, Star has an alternate form for each emotion on her emotional spectrum. Each one reflects something she feels, something she can’t always say. Frost… is what sadness looks like when it becomes too much to hold inside.

She activated it today over something small. I fell, and my ice cream slipped from my hand and hit the ground. It wasn’t important. It shouldn’t have been. But for her, it was enough.

Star has been cold—not just in the way she acts, but in a way you can feel before you even see her. There’s something like a frost aura around her now, something that spreads without asking, something that makes people shiver the moment they get too close. It got so bad that Lucy AI had to disable every AC unit within a 150 meter radius, just to keep the entire sector from freezing over. Even then, the cold doesn’t really leave. It just settles, like it belongs to her now.

And still, she tries.

She looks at me the same way, forces that same small, careful smile, like she always does, like she thinks if she holds it long enough, I might believe it. Like she thinks she can still protect me from seeing what’s really there. But she isn’t fine. I know she isn’t. I knew she wouldn’t be.

Star is slowly breaking apart.

But that isn’t the part that scares me the most.

What scares me is the thought that one day… she might decide to stop holding on entirely.

She spends hours standing beside Stitch, just watching him. Not speaking, not moving much, just… staying there. Like if she leaves, something worse might happen. Like her presence alone is the only thing keeping him here.

He looks… peace.

Half dead, half alive.

The left side of his body is gone in a way that doesn’t feel survivable. The limbs—his arm, his leg—they’re barely there, fragile enough that it feels like they could come apart if touched the wrong way. The damage from the Furnace incident never really left him. Charlie’s intervention is the only reason he’s still here at all. His left arm and leg were blown off, and somehow she made his body believe everything was still intact, tricking it into continuing, into surviving.

But that kind of thing doesn’t reach Star.

You can’t trick her into believing everything is fine. Not with words, not with illusions, not with anything we have.

She sees things exactly as they are.

And all she feels…  
is sadness.
