# 25/04/2025

25/05/2025

Weather: Sunny

Mood: ███████

I woke up to something I didn’t think I would ever hear again.

Good news.

Stitch woke up.

For a moment, I didn’t move, like my body needed time to understand what those words meant, like hope itself had become something unfamiliar. And then it hit me all at once, sharp and overwhelming, and I ran. I didn’t think, didn’t stop, didn’t even breathe properly—I just ran straight to Star’s room and banged on the door like everything depended on it.

Because it did.

From the other side, her voice came through.

Cold. Empty. Distant.

“What.”

Just one word, and it felt like I was already too late.

But I didn’t stop. I didn’t hesitate. I shouted it, louder than I ever have, like if I didn’t say it with everything I had, it wouldn’t reach her.

“STITCH’S AWAKE.”

There was a loud bang from inside. Something solid, something sudden—like she hit the wall, like the world inside that room cracked open all at once.

Then the door opened.

And we ran.

Through the halls, past frozen walls and silent corridors, like we were escaping something, like if we slowed down even for a second, this would all disappear again. The cold didn’t matter, the distance didn’t matter, nothing mattered except getting there.

The medbay doors.

And inside—

him.

Stitch.

Alive.

Not broken. Not fading. Not lost.

Just… there.

Resting on the bed like he had only been sleeping, like none of this had ever happened.

We stopped.

For the first time in days—weeks—everything stopped.

He opened his eyes.

And he didn’t look at me.

He looked at her.

At Star.

At what she had become.

An angel consumed by grief, standing in silence. The azure dress still clung to her, magnificent and heavy, roses wrapped around her body like armor that had protected her for too long. The crown of vines rested upon her head, something both beautiful and binding, something that told the story of everything she had endured just to reach this moment.

She said nothing.

Not a single word.

She just moved forward—

and hugged him.

Tightly.

Like if she let go, he would disappear again.

And then… she cried.

Not quietly. Not softly. But in a way that felt like everything she had been holding back was finally breaking free. Layer by layer, the things that defined her grief began to fall apart. The dress cracked and faded, the roses around her body withered and broke, petals falling like fragments of something that no longer needed to exist.

The Queen of Frost…

was letting go.

“Star… can you loosen a bit?” Stitch said, his voice still weak, still human, still here.

She let go.

Slowly.

Like it was the hardest thing she had ever done.

“Stitch… I thought I lost you…” she muttered, her voice fragile, unfamiliar, like something that hadn’t been used in a long time. Fifteen days of silence, of distance, of becoming something else entirely—and now, finally, she was here again.

And just like that…

she came back.

The shadow over her eyes was gone. The golden light returned, soft at first, then brighter, steadier, like it had never truly left—only hidden beneath everything else. The cold faded, the frost loosened its grip on the world around her, and that warmth… that quiet, steady warmth she always carried… came back with her.

The same Star.

The same light.

The same glow that made everything feel a little less heavy.

And this time—

it was real.

People began to gather. Voices filled the medbay, relief, disbelief, something close to joy spreading through the space that had been silent for so long. They cheered for her, for him, for both of them—for something that almost didn’t make it back.

And I stood there.

Watching.

Breathing.

Holding onto something I almost lost too.

Maybe this is where I stop.

Maybe this is where things finally rest.

Or maybe…  
 this is just the part where the light comes back—

after everything that tried to take it away.
