# 25/07/2025

25/07/2025  
 Weather: Heavy Rain  
 Mood: █████████

I’m back.

Not in the way people return after being gone, not suddenly, not loudly—but in a quieter way, like I never really stopped writing, just kept everything where no one else could see it. I’ve been keeping these journals through all the days before this, holding onto every moment, every change, every small piece that felt like it mattered too much to lose. But now… they aren’t just mine anymore. I have to make them public. Not because I want attention, not because I’m angry or resentful, but because people need to see this. They need to understand what happened, what’s still happening, and what almost disappeared without anyone noticing in time.

Star’s recovery has been fast—faster than I expected, faster than anyone expected—and it only proves what we already knew. Charlie is exceptional. Not just skilled, not just reliable, but something beyond that, something precise enough to bring someone back from the edge and make it look possible. For humans, for Starladians… it doesn’t seem to matter. If anyone could do it, it was always going to be her.

And Star… she didn’t come back quietly.

After the injury, she’s been more active than ever, moving through the facility like she’s trying to make up for lost time, like standing still isn’t something she allows herself anymore. Even with the damage—her leg not fully healed, her voice taken from her in a way that words can’t easily replace—she still keeps going. She walks with a cane when she has to, leans on others when she can’t, and sometimes… she lets me carry her. Most of the time, actually. She doesn’t complain about it, doesn’t hesitate, just accepts it like it’s natural, like trust has always been that simple for her.

She speaks differently now. Not with her voice, but with gestures, with small movements, with the way she points, signals, looks at you just long enough for you to understand. And somehow… it still feels like her. Nothing important was lost, not really. If anything, it feels clearer, like she doesn’t need words to be heard.

Star has been more present than ever.

And that’s what I can’t understand.

I still don’t know who would do this to her.

Because she’s… not just kind, not just good in the way people try to be. She’s something else entirely. An angel, literally and not, someone who gives without thinking twice, someone who brings warmth into places that don’t deserve it and still makes them feel like they do. She helps everyone. No exceptions. No hesitation. She gives people something to hold onto, something steady, something real.

Without her… this place changes.

It becomes quieter in the wrong way, heavier in a way that doesn’t lift. A place that already carries too much begins to feel like it’s sinking under its own weight. A hole filled with something close to depression, something close to darkness.

No offense, Director Lucious.

But it’s true.

Star fills something here that no system, no protocol, no structure ever could. Something human. Something fragile. Something that holds people together without them realizing it.

Family.

That’s what she is.

Not just to one person, not just to a few—  
 to everyone.

She’s like a key that fits without forcing, something that belongs wherever it’s needed, something that doesn’t replace what’s broken but makes it easier to carry. People are comfortable around her in ways they don’t question. They trust her without needing a reason. They remember her, every single one of them, because she never leaves anyone behind.

Not once.

So I keep asking myself the same thing, over and over, like if I repeat it enough, I might finally understand.

Why.

Why would anyone do this to her.
