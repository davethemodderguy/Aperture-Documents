# 27/03/2025

27/03/2025

Weather: █████████

Mood: Uneasy

Star is… normal.

Or at least, that’s what it looks like from a distance. Something about her has settled, not in a way that feels like healing, but in a way that feels quiet, controlled, like everything has been pushed down just enough to stop spilling over. Most of the time now, she stays locked inside her room, alone with thoughts I can’t bring myself to imagine, thoughts that sit behind a door no one else can open.

That door doesn’t respond to anyone. Not Director Lucious, even with his “absolute” access that’s supposed to open anything. Not Charlie, no matter how much she might want to reach her. Not The Contractor, who rarely leaves her stand unless something truly matters to her. None of them can get through.

Just me.

Only me.

And I don’t know why.

After these past days, she’s still… herself, in pieces that haven’t completely disappeared. The frost around her hasn’t gone away. It lingers, quiet and constant, freezing the roses near her without hesitation. Petals turn brittle, stems stiffen under the cold, but she doesn’t react. Maybe because she knows they’ll be fine when they thaw, or maybe because she just doesn’t have the energy to care anymore.

Her room is covered in it. Cold pressed into every surface, frost stretching across walls, floor, air—everything touched by that same quiet sadness. It should feel unbearable to stand in there, like something that pushes you out the moment you step inside.

But for me… it doesn’t.

I feel warm.

Not because of my clothes, not because the cold isn’t real, but because of something else I can’t fully explain, something that stays between us even now, even like this.

She still leaves sometimes, just to get food, water—just enough to keep going. But she always comes back, always retreats into that room, into that silence that seems to understand her better than anything else right now.

So I stay with her when I can.

I tell her things. Small things. Old things. I read her stories about the Blacksite from before she ever arrived, about a time she never saw. I tell her about my own pain, things I don’t usually say out loud, hoping that maybe if she hears it, she won’t feel so alone in hers.

And somehow… it helps.

Her smile isn’t as forced anymore. It doesn’t look like it’s held together by effort alone. It stays a little longer, feels a little more real, like something fragile that’s trying to come back instead of something pretending to exist.

But she’s still in Frost.

Still cold.

Still sad.

And I can’t stop thinking about how easily this could become something worse.

I just hope… it doesn’t escalate.
