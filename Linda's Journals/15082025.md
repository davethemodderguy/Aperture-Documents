# 15/08/2025

15/08/2025  
 Mood: Excited  
 Weather: Perfect

Star made a plan.

Not just any plan—not something rushed, not something desperate—but something precise, something *hers*. Careful, deliberate, built on understanding rather than anger. And somehow, despite everything she’s been through… it worked.

According to Rose’s intel, her brother was being held at Urbanshade HQ.

A prisoner.

A leverage point.

A mistake.

Because holding someone like that doesn’t just give you control—

it gives us a direction.

Once we confirmed the target, everything moved quickly. Preparations, assignments, movement paths—everyone knew what they had to do. There was no hesitation, no second-guessing. This wasn’t just about a mission anymore. It was about ending something that had already gone too far.

But then—

something unexpected happened.

Director Lucious received a call.

Not from within.

From outside.

Innovations. Inc.

They reached out directly, something no one anticipated, something that immediately raised suspicion. According to them, Urbanshade had been a problem for a long time. Persistent. Disruptive. Untouchable in ways that made them dangerous to leave alone.

They wanted an alliance.

Temporary.

Focused.

To eliminate a shared threat.

At first, no one trusted it. Not fully. Not without question. But the timing, the intent, the alignment of goals—it all fit too well to ignore.

And eventually…

Director Lucious agreed.

At exactly 2 PM, we were in position.

Operators lined up, silent, ready. Director Lucious at the front. Charlie and Stitch beside him. And Star…

Star stood with us.

Not behind.

Not protected.

With us.

The air felt still, like the world itself was waiting. All eyes fixed on the gates ahead, closed for now, but not for long.

Because the moment they opened—

everything would begin.

We waited.

Seconds stretched.

And then—

they opened.

And we moved.

No hesitation.

No warning.

We stormed in.

What followed is… hard to piece together cleanly. A firestorm of movement, sound, impact—everything blending into something chaotic but controlled. Gunfire echoed, footsteps collided, commands overlapped. The air itself felt heavy, thick with motion and intent.

But through all of it—

everyone fought.

Not just us.

Innovations. Inc. kept their word.

Their operators moved alongside ours, coordinated, efficient, no signs of betrayal, no hesitation in their actions. For that moment, at least…

we were aligned.

We pushed forward.

Room by room.

Step by step.

Until we found him.

Rose’s brother.

Alive.

Barely, but alive.

And that was enough.

We freed him.

No delay.

No second thought.

And then we moved to exfiltrate.

It should have ended there.

Clean.

Complete.

But it didn’t.

Because as we were about to leave—

he appeared.

Mr. Shade.

Standing in our path like he had been waiting for this exact moment. Calm. Composed. That expression on his face… the kind that doesn’t belong in the middle of a collapsing operation.

Like he had something to say.

Like he believed we would listen.

He opened his mouth—

And never got the chance.

Three shots.

Perfectly timed.

Perfectly placed.

One from Charlie.

One from Stitch.

One from Director Lucious.

He fell before a single word could leave him.

And just like that—

it was over.

We exited.

No pursuit.

No interruption.

Just silence returning where chaos had been.

Everything after that… feels distant.

Blurred.

Not because it wasn’t important, but because everything finally stopped moving all at once, and the weight of it caught up to me. I was drained. Completely. Like the moment we stepped out, my body realized it didn’t have to keep going anymore.

So this is shorter.

Not because there’s nothing to say—

but because I don’t have anything left to give right now.

Still…

I can say this.

Star recovered.

Rose is free.

Her brother is safe.

And for the first time in a long time—

everything feels like it’s back where it should be.

Or at least…

nothing bad is happening.

And for now—

that’s enough.
