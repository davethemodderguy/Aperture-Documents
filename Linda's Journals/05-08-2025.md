 Mood: Scared  
 Weather: Stormy

The storm didn’t just stay outside today.

It felt like it followed her.

Star, who used to be the quiet light in every corner of this place, the one who softened everything without trying, is… breaking herself apart in ways I don’t know how to stop. Not loudly, not in ways people would immediately notice—but in small, continuous fractures that keep spreading the longer no one stops her.

Today, it was something simple.

We were just stamping paperwork together. Nothing urgent. Nothing heavy. Just routine, something ordinary enough to feel safe. And for a moment, it almost felt like things could be normal again.

Then she coughed.

And there was blood.

It shouldn’t have looked like that.

Her blood—if it could even be called that—was always pale, almost white, something that never felt like it belonged to something meant to die. But this time… it was different. Tinted red. Faint, but unmistakable.

Something human.

Something wrong.

Something I was afraid I’d see one day.

I told her to stop.

I kept telling her.

Over and over, more urgently each time, asking her to sit down, to rest, to just *pause* for once. To let her body catch up to everything she’s been forcing it through.

She refused.

Not casually. Not dismissively.

She refused like it was the only thing she could do.

Like stopping would mean something worse than collapsing.

And five minutes later—

she did.

It wasn’t sudden in a dramatic way. No warning, no cry, no struggle. She just… fell. Like something inside her finally gave up trying to keep up with her will. Like her body decided it couldn’t follow her anymore.

I caught her too late.

Diagnostics came in faster than I could think.

The results didn’t feel real.

Residual damage. Leftover from the attack. Something that didn’t heal, something that stayed hidden, something that kept working against her from the inside.

Killing her.

Second by second.

Quietly.

Relentlessly.

They said… without intervention…

she had five days.

Five.

I told her.

Or maybe I didn’t need to.

Maybe she already knew.

Because when we brought up surgery—when we told her there was still a way to stop it, still a way to pull her back from this—she refused again.

The same way.

With everything she had left.

Like her life wasn’t something worth holding onto.

Like it didn’t belong to her anymore.

And I didn’t know what else to do.

So I did the one thing I never thought I would.

I threatened to end my own.

The words didn’t feel real when I said them. They didn’t belong to me. But they were the only thing that reached her, the only thing that broke through whatever she had locked herself inside.

She looked at me.

Really looked.

And she agreed.

Reluctantly.

Like choosing between two things she didn’t want to lose.

The surgery went well.

Better than expected.

Her body responded, healed more than we thought it could, stabilized in ways that gave us time we didn’t have before. Physically… she’s still here. Still holding on.

But she hasn’t come back.

Not fully.

She’s still in that form.

“Betrayal.”

The white dress. The unmoving blade. The shattered halo. The missing wing. The tears that don’t melt.

It’s still there.

And it hurts more than anything else.

Because I can fix a wound.

I can fight for her body.

But I can’t reach that part of her.

I keep asking myself the same questions, over and over, like if I repeat them enough, I’ll find an answer that makes sense.

Why wasn’t I there?

Why didn’t I stop it?

Why did I let this happen to her?

Why do I only ever arrive when it’s already too late?

And now there’s another question I can’t escape.

One that stays, no matter how much I try to ignore it.

Will Star wake up tomorrow?
