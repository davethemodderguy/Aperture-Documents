# 15/04/2025

15/04/2025

Weather: Overcast

Mood: ███████

I went to Star’s room today.

She let me in.

That alone felt different, like something small had shifted, like a door that had been closing for days finally paused instead of shutting completely. When I stepped inside, the cold met me the same way it always does now—sharp, biting, settling into my skin like it wanted to stay—but I ignored it. I looked at her.

She looked the same.

The azure roses still covered her body like armor, wrapping around her arms, her torso, her legs, blooming in that unnatural cold as if they belonged there more than anything alive should. The frost around her hadn’t softened either. It still hurt to be near, still pressed against me in a way that made every second feel heavier than it should have.

But this time… she wasn’t looking at me.

She was looking at the table.

And on it, set carefully like it had been placed with purpose, was a grafting knife.

One that Stitch made for her.

I could still see the tag on the hilt, untouched, unmoved despite everything that’s happened. “Happy Birthday Star\!” It felt out of place now, like a piece of a different time that somehow survived into this one without changing.

She picked it up.

Slowly.

Carefully.

Like she already knew exactly what she was going to do with it.

And then she raised it… and pressed it against her neck.

Everything in me reacted before I could even think. I moved, fast enough that I don’t remember crossing the space between us, just the moment I reached her, grabbing her wrist, trying to pull the knife away before it could go any further. I called her name, over and over, not even sure what I was saying, just trying to bring her back, trying to make her hear me through whatever she was lost in.

We struggled.

Not violently, not like enemies, but like two people caught on opposite sides of something neither of us could fully control. I tried to force the blade away from her, tried to break her focus, tried anything that might stop her from finishing what she started.

And then—

she let go.

So suddenly I didn’t expect it.

The knife slipped.

It slid through my hand.

And then there was pain.

Sharp. Immediate. Real.

Blood began to pour from my palm, warm against the cold that surrounded us, too fast, too much. My grip failed, my body followed, and before I could steady myself, I hit the ground. The frozen floor didn’t feel solid—it felt distant, like I was already starting to lose sense of where I was.

The blood spread, dark against the frost, moving in a way that felt wrong in a place like this.

I could feel myself fading.

The cold, the pain, the sudden emptiness in my head—it all started to blur together. I thought, just for a second, that this might be it, that maybe I pushed too far this time.

And then—

something held my hand.

Warm.

Not the kind of warmth I remembered, but something gentler, something that didn’t fight the cold but existed within it.

It was Star.

A soft green glow formed around my palm, faint but steady, spreading through the wound like something alive, something careful. The pain eased, the blood slowed, and before I could even fully process it… it was gone.

My hand was healed.

Completely.

I looked at her.

She was already looking at me.

For a moment, neither of us moved. The frost didn’t press as hard, the room didn’t feel as distant, and something—something small but undeniable—felt like it had come back.

And then…

she smiled.

Not forced.

Not fragile.

Not something placed there just to protect me.

A real smile.

The kind I hadn’t seen in weeks.

The kind I thought was already gone.

And in that moment, something inside me came back too.

Hope.

Just a little.

Just enough.

So I’ll hold onto it.

For her.

For Stitch.

For whatever is still left of the light she’s trying to find again.

Let’s hope… for both of them.
