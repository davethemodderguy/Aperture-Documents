Star is the same today.

At least… that’s what it looks like on the surface. The same movements, the same quiet way of helping, the same presence that fills a space before anyone even realizes she’s there. If I didn’t look too closely, if I didn’t already know her the way I do, I could almost believe nothing is wrong anymore—that everything has settled, that she’s simply… back.

But she’s covering something.

Not perfectly. Not enough to hide it completely. Just enough that most people wouldn’t question it, wouldn’t stop to notice the small inconsistencies, the moments that don’t quite align. It’s subtle, the kind of thing you only see if you’re already looking for it.

Star’s presence isn’t just more frequent now.

It’s… repeating.

I don’t know how else to describe it. It’s like she appears where she’s needed, exactly when she’s needed, but in ways that feel too precise, too immediate. Someone drops a pen, and she’s already there before it fully hits the ground. Someone hesitates for a second, and she’s beside them in that exact moment, like she was waiting for it to happen.

Small things.

Insignificant things.

Things no one would question on their own.

But when you start putting them together… it feels different.

It feels like she’s not just moving through the facility anymore. It feels like she’s… overlapping with it, like she exists in more than one place in a way that doesn’t fully make sense, like she’s making sure no moment goes unanswered, no matter how small.

And no one else seems to notice.

Or maybe they do… and they just don’t think about it long enough to feel how strange it is.

That’s all I can see.

Or maybe… that’s all she let me see.

Because if this is her trying to cover something up—

then whatever she’s hiding must be something she doesn’t want anyone, even me, to understand.
