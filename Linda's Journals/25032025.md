This is Linda.

Star has been quiet in a way that doesn’t just sit in the air, but lingers, like something heavy that refuses to leave. Ever since Stitch fell into that coma, she’s been pulling away—not suddenly, not enough for most people to point it out right away, but slowly, carefully, like she’s afraid that if she lets go too quickly, whatever is holding her together might fall apart. She keeps her distance now, from everyone, even Charlie… her own big sister. There’s a space between them that wasn’t there before, something unspoken, something neither of them seems able to cross.

There are only two places she still goes without hesitation: her garden, and me. I’ve been her friend since day one, since the moment she got here, and I thought I understood her, or at least the way her light worked. But I’ve never seen her like this before. Director Lucious has to reassure her again and again that Stitch is fine, that there’s nothing to worry about, but the way she listens… it feels like the words reach her, just not enough to settle in her heart.

She used to be the light of this facility, not in any official way, just something you felt when she was around. She made things easier, softer, like people could breathe a little better just by standing near her. Now she can’t even smile once unless I’m around, and even then, it doesn’t feel real. It’s just a small, careful thing she puts on, like a mask meant to reassure me more than anything else, quietly saying she’s fine without ever meaning it.

She’s always cared about me, more than most people would. I’ve seen it in the way she stands by me, in the way she doesn’t hesitate. Considering what she did to Operator Daniel █████████ just for bullying me, I know she protects her friends with everything she has, no matter how far she has to go. Nothing unusual has happened to her, at least nothing anyone can officially point to, nothing that can be written down as a problem. But something is changing, slowly and quietly, and I can’t ignore it no matter how much I try.

I just hope Stitch wakes up soon. Because right now, Star is no longer a star. She’s still there, still trying to be, but she’s fading little by little, like a light that doesn’t know how to keep shining when the one it was shining for isn’t there to see it.
