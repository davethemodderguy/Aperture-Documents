Mood: Concerned  
Weather: Sunny

Today started like any other.

Star woke up, walked into the cafeteria, moved through the morning like she always does now—quiet, gentle, present. Nothing felt out of place at first. Nothing felt wrong.

Until I noticed.

Her eyepatch was gone.

Not misplaced in a careless way, not forgotten like something unimportant—but gone from where it should have been. The small, stitched piece that Charlie and Stitch made for her, woven together with care, with patience, with something deeper than just function. It wasn’t just something to cover her eye.

It was love.

And now… it wasn’t there.

She covered her left eye with her hand instead.

Carefully. Almost instinctively.

Like she didn’t want anyone to see.

Something about that… unsettled me.

Maybe it’s nothing, I told myself. I don’t fully understand how Starladian bodies work, what they can endure, what they recover from, what they hide. Maybe it’s normal. Maybe it’s just sensitivity, or discomfort, or something that will pass.

But I couldn’t shake it.

Concern comes naturally to me now.

When I asked her to lift her hand, she hesitated.

And then…

she teared up.

Just slightly.

But enough.

Like I had asked her to reveal something she didn’t want to lose, something she wasn’t ready to face. And I understood that feeling more than I wanted to. If I lost something like that—something tied to people I care about, something made with that much meaning—I would’ve cried too.

But her tears…

they weren’t normal.

I’ve seen them before.

Transparent. Cold. Almost weightless. Carrying that faint, quiet healing effect that never fully explains itself.

This is her blood.

And that thought—

that realization—

made everything feel heavier.

I asked again.

More gently this time.

She told me it hurts when air touches her eye. Not unbearable, not enough to stop her—but enough that she noticed it, enough that she chose to hide it.

And still…

she lifted her hand.

For me.

And what I saw—

was wrong.

A crack.

Not a wound. Not something bleeding, not something broken in the way I understand injuries.

A crack.

Like glass.

Starting from the corner of her eye, stretching inward toward the center of her iris, thin but unmistakable. Something fragile. Something that shouldn’t exist in something living.

I’ve seen damaged eyes before.

Pierced ones.

Destroyed ones.

Gone.

But this…

this was different.

Her eye wasn’t gone.

It was… fractured.

Like something delicate had been struck and never fully repaired.

For a moment, I didn’t know what to think.

Didn’t know what to say.

Didn’t know what I _could_ say.

So I did the only thing I could—

I set it aside.

Not because it didn’t matter.

But because right now, something else did.

We needed to find her eyepatch.

So we went looking.

Together.

Through the Blacksite, retracing every step she took, every place she passed through, every small corner where something could have been left behind. It felt strange, searching for something so small in a place so large—but it mattered.

It mattered to her.

So it mattered to me.

And eventually—

we found it.

Under her bed.

Slipped just out of sight, tucked into that small space where things disappear without meaning to. It must have fallen when she leaned over, unnoticed, left behind without her realizing.

I picked it up.

It felt… simple.

Just a piece of cloth.

Woven.

Stitched.

Nothing extraordinary in form.

But everything about it… carried something more.

I placed it back where it belonged.

Gently.

Carefully.

And when she wore it again…

she looked like herself.

Not completely, not perfectly—but closer.

And I realized something else.

Around her neck…

a small golden bell.

I hadn’t noticed it before.

It rested there quietly, catching light in a soft way, something new, something out of place in the best way possible.

I asked her about it.

She told me it was a gift.

From Rose.

And for a moment…

I just smiled.

Because after everything—

that still remained.

She shook her head slightly as she walked, and the bell let out soft, gentle chimes that followed her down the hallway, light and steady, like something small that refused to be taken away.

And I listened to it fade…

hoping it would always come back.
