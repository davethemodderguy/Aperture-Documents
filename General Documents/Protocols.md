# Protocols

**APERTURE INDUSTRIES**  
 **SITE-WIDE PROTOCOL ARCHIVE — INTRODUCTION**  
 **Classification: Internal / All Personnel Mandatory**

---

### **I. PURPOSE**

**Protocols** are the immediate-response backbone of Aperture Industries.

They exist to convert complex, high-risk situations into **clear, executable commands** that every employee can understand and follow without hesitation.

In an environment where seconds determine survival, protocols remove uncertainty.

---

### **II. FUNCTION**

Protocols serve as:

* **Rapid Communication Tools**  
   A single phrase conveys a full operational directive  
* **Standardized Response Systems**  
   Every individual responds the same way to the same trigger  
* **Authority Overrides**  
   Active protocols supersede standard duties and chain-of-command delays

---

### **III. DELIVERY SYSTEM**

All protocols are:

* Broadcast through the **PA System**  
* Recognized by **predefined keywords**  
* Enforced across **all divisions and personnel levels**

Upon announcement:

* Immediate compliance is required  
* Delay or confusion is considered a critical failure

---

### **IV. UNIVERSAL APPLICATION**

Site-Wide Protocols apply to:

* **GENERAL operations**  
* **C.O.R.E divisions**  
* **N.O.S.T divisions**

No department is exempt.

This ensures:

* Coordinated response across the entire facility  
* Elimination of conflicting actions between divisions  
* Unified control during emergencies

---

### **V. WHY PROTOCOLS EXIST AS A SEPARATE SYSTEM**

Protocols are not categorized under standard operations because:

* They are **not situational records** (unlike Event Logs)  
* They are **not assets or structures** (unlike General Documents)  
* They are **not tools or systems** (unlike C.O.R.E Equipment)

They are **instructions under pressure**.

Separating them ensures:

* Immediate accessibility  
* Memorization efficiency  
* Zero ambiguity during activation

---

### **VI. COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENT**

All personnel must:

* Memorize all protocol keywords  
* Understand corresponding actions  
* Execute without hesitation when activated

Failure to comply may result in:

* Loss of containment  
* Personnel casualties  
* Facility termination

---

### **VII. OPERATIONAL PHILOSOPHY**

In Aperture Industries, hesitation is more dangerous than error.

Protocols exist so you don’t have to think—

**only respond.**
