# Z-565-A "Ocean of Souls"

APERTURE INDUSTRIES — N.O.S.T DIVISION  
 ENTITY DOSSIER: Z-565-A  
 Designation: Ocean of Souls  
 Classification: Restricted / Z-Class

---

I. OVERVIEW

Z-565-A, designated as the **Ocean of Souls**, is a vast metaphysical expanse surrounding the primary Banlands territory (Z-565).

Despite the term “ocean,” the entity does not conform to conventional liquid or physical oceanic properties. Instead, it functions as a dimensional medium composed of accumulated soul entities existing in a suspended, non-corporeal state.

Z-565-A serves as the primary holding and transitional environment for all deceased entities within Banlands jurisdiction.

---

II. STRUCTURAL PROPERTIES

Observed characteristics include:

* Apparent infinite spatial extent (unconfirmed boundaries)  
* Non-Newtonian, non-fluid composition  
* Aggregated soul-mass density with variable clustering patterns  
* Lack of consistent buoyancy, pressure, or physical interaction mechanics

Entities within Z-565-A do not “sink” or “float” in a traditional sense. Instead, they remain suspended within the collective medium, influenced by unknown metaphysical forces.

---

III. FUNCTIONAL ROLE

Z-565-A operates as the central processing layer within the Banlands soul cycle:

1. Souls enter Z-565-A upon death or transfer  
2. Entities remain in a passive or semi-conscious state  
3. Souls are retrieved by Ferryman entities (Z-565-B: Lady Death)  
4. Outcomes are determined post-retrieval:  
   * Revival (conditional)  
   * Treeification (default outcome)

Z-565-A itself does not appear to execute judgment; it functions as a containment and aggregation field.

---

IV. SOUL STATE BEHAVIOR

Documented behaviors of soul entities within Z-565-A include:

* Reduced or absent sensory response  
* Minimal autonomous movement  
* Gradual clustering into dense formations  
* Occasional reactive disturbances during large-scale retrieval events

There is no confirmed communication between soul entities. No structured social or hierarchical interactions have been observed.

---

V. TREEIFICATION INTERFACE

Z-565-A is directly connected to the **Tree of the Banlands** via an unknown transfer mechanism.

Treeification process:

* Souls are extracted from Z-565-A  
* Transported to the Tree interface  
* Absorbed and utilized as growth input

The purpose of the Tree remains unverified, though it is presumed to be integral to Banlands environmental or systemic stability.

---

VI. FERRYMAN INTERACTION

Z-565-A is actively navigated and accessed by Ferryman entity Z-565-B (Lady Death).

Operational notes:

* Retrieval is selective and controlled  
* Ferry routes appear to be instinctive or pre-defined without visible instrumentation  
* Tokens issued by Aperture Industries serve as compensation triggers for revival operations

The Devil has shown no interference in Ferryman traversal across Z-565-A, indicating mutual operational tolerance.

---

VII. OBSERVED ANOMALIES

* Soul density fluctuations without external input  
* Apparent “currents” or directional flows lacking physical cause  
* Regions of higher or lower responsiveness among soul clusters  
* Unverified depth layers or stratification within the medium

No confirmed entities of independent origin have been identified residing permanently within Z-565-A apart from aggregated souls.

---

VIII. RISK ASSESSMENT

Current Risk Level: MODERATE

Primary risks include:

* Unknown metaphysical properties affecting personnel exposure  
* Potential for loss of physical form upon direct contact without Ferryman mediation  
* Instability during large-scale retrieval events  
* Unpredictable reactions during dimensional interference

Mitigating factors:

* Restricted access enforced  
* Ferryman-controlled traversal pathways  
* Established cooperative framework with Banlands authority

---

IX. RELATION TO BANLANDS SYSTEM

Z-565-A is a core component of Banlands infrastructure, functioning as:

* The primary intake reservoir for souls  
* A staging layer between death and outcome determination  
* A stabilizing mass that supports the broader Banlands cycle

Without Z-565-A, the Banlands operational model would collapse.

---

X. STATUS

Operational State: ACTIVE  
 Stability: STABLE (HIGH VOLUME)  
 Accessibility: RESTRICTED / FERRYMAN ONLY  
 External Interaction: PROHIBITED WITHOUT AUTHORIZATION
