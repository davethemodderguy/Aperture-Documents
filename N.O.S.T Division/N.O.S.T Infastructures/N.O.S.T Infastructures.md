**APERTURE INDUSTRIES**  
**N.O.S.T INFRASTRUCTURE — INTRODUCTION**  
**Classification: Internal / Restricted**

---

### I. PURPOSE

**N.O.S.T Infrastructure** defines the systems and structures that allow Aperture Industries to **operate beyond Earth**.

These are not traditional facilities.

They are built to exist in environments where:

- Gravity may not apply
- Space may not be stable
- Distance may not be measurable

---

### II. ROLE

N.O.S.T Infrastructure enables:

- Establishment of operational presence in non-Earth locations
- Stabilization of anomalous environments
- Safe deployment and retrieval of personnel
- Maintenance of cross-realm access and communication

It turns unknown territory into **controlled operational ground**.

### IV. DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

N.O.S.T Infrastructure is built under extreme uncertainty:

**1. Adapt to the Environment**  
Structures must adjust to unstable or shifting conditions.

**2. Anchor Reality**  
Where possible, systems impose stability onto surrounding space.

**3. Remain Independent**  
Infrastructure must operate without reliance on Earth-based support.

---

### V. OPERATIONAL ROLE

N.O.S.T Infrastructure ensures:

- Safe access to discovered realms
- Sustained presence beyond Earth
- Controlled interaction with non-terrestrial environments

Without it:

- Portals become unstable
- Expeditions become one-way
- Communication is lost

---

### VI. DIFFERENCE FROM C.O.R.E INFRASTRUCTURE

**C.O.R.E Infrastructure:**

- Built to control and maintain facilities on Earth

**N.O.S.T Infrastructure:**

- Built to survive and function outside of it

One maintains control.

The other makes presence possible.
