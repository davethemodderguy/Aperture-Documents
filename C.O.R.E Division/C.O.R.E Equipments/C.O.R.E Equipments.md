**

**APERTURE INDUSTRIES  
C.O.R.E EQUIPMENT ARCHIVE — INTRODUCTION  
Classification: Internal**

---

### I. PURPOSE

The C.O.R.E Equipment Archive exists to document the tools, weapons, and systems that allow Aperture Industries to interact with, control, and survive anomalies.

If entities are the threat,  
then equipment is the difference between containment and catastrophe.

---

### II. ROLE OF C.O.R.E EQUIPMENT

Under the Central Operations & Research Executive (C.O.R.E), equipment is not designed for conventional use.

Every tool within this archive is built with one purpose:

To function where normal technology fails.

This includes:

- Direct interaction with anomalous entities
    
- Operation within unstable environments
    
- Execution of containment and neutralization procedures
    
- Survival under conditions that violate standard physics
    

---

### III. DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

C.O.R.E equipment is developed under three principles:

1. Adaptation Over Perfection  
Anomalies are unpredictable. Equipment must adjust faster than they evolve.

2. Controlled Risk  
Many tools utilize unstable or partially understood phenomena.  
Failure is accounted for—but never eliminated.

3. Superiority Through Integration  
Technology is often combined with anomalous properties to achieve results impossible through conventional engineering.

---

### IV. CLASSIFICATION OF EQUIPMENT

C.O.R.E equipment typically falls under the following categories:

- Containment Systems  
    Devices used to restrain or isolate entities
    
- Field Equipment  
    Tools issued to operatives during capture and retrieval missions
    
- Weapon Systems  
    Anomaly-grade weaponry designed for neutralization
    
- Support Infrastructure  
    Systems that sustain blacksite operations (power, AI cores, monitoring systems)
    

---

### V. WHY A SEPARATE ARCHIVE EXISTS

C.O.R.E equipment cannot be categorized under GENERAL due to its nature.

Unlike standard infrastructure:

- It interacts directly with anomalies
    
- It may exhibit anomalous behavior itself
    
- Mishandling can result in immediate loss of containment
    

Separating this archive ensures:

- Restricted access
    
- Specialized documentation
    
- Reduced risk of misuse
    

---

### VI. OPERATIONAL SIGNIFICANCE

C.O.R.E does not rely on strength alone.

It relies on precision tools designed for impossible problems.

Without these systems:

- Containment fails
    
- Research halts
    
- Aperture loses control
    

---

### VII. FINAL NOTE

An anomaly breaks the rules.

C.O.R.E equipment rewrites them—just enough to survive.
