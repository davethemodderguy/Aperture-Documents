I. OVERVIEW

The following entries document known variants of Entity Z-283. Each variant exhibits unique behavioral, morphological, and electromagnetic deviations while retaining core characteristics associated with the primary entity classification.

Variants are designated using suffix identifiers appended to the base entity ID (e.g., Z-283-B, Z-283-P).

---

## II. Z-283-B — “BLITZ”

Designation: Z-283-B  
Codename: Blitz  
Variant Type: High-Velocity Aggressor

---

A. IDENTIFICATION

Z-283-B is the first recorded variant of Z-283, typically manifesting approximately ███ minutes after initial Z-283 sighting.

Morphological traits include:

- Facial structure resembling Chauliodus species
    
- Three luminescent eyes located within the left orbital region
    
- Right eye socket absent/void
    

---

B. BEHAVIORAL PROFILE

- Exhibits extreme acceleration capabilities
    
- Demonstrates immediate pursuit upon detection of targets
    
- Capable of closing distance before standard human reaction thresholds
    

Engagement Risk:

- Survival probability drops to near zero upon auditory confirmation of echolocation
    

---

C. ELECTROMAGNETIC EFFECTS

- Causes significantly amplified disruption compared to baseline Z-283
    
- Lighting systems exhibit extended flickering durations
    
- Electrical interference persists for approximately 2× longer than standard variants
    

---

D. OPERATIONAL DIRECTIVE

- Immediate concealment is mandatory upon identification
    
- Avoid exposure to open corridors or line-of-sight environments
    
- Do not rely on electronic warning systems
    

---

## III. Z-283-P — “PINKIE”

Designation: Z-283-P  
Codename: Pinkie  
Variant Type: Low-Speed, Low-EM Signature Entity

---

A. IDENTIFICATION

- Distinct vibrant pink facial coloration
    
- Presence of an esca (bioluminescent lure)
    
- Similar general morphology to baseline Z-283
    

---

B. BEHAVIORAL PROFILE

- Reduced locomotion speed compared to other variants
    
- More predictable movement patterns
    
- Retains echolocation-based navigation
    

---

C. ELECTROMAGNETIC PROFILE

- Does not induce electrical disruption or circuit overload
    
- No associated flickering or system interference
    

Operational Implication:

- Personnel cannot rely on electrical anomalies as a warning indicator
    

---

D. AUDITORY SIGNATURE

- Echolocation scream described as highly piercing and distinctive
    
- Audible detection is a primary cue for awareness
    

---

E. OPERATIONAL DIRECTIVE

- Maintain auditory vigilance at all times
    
- Do not rely on environmental electrical feedback
    
- React to sound-based detection rather than visual anomalies
    

---

## IV. Z-283-F — “FROGER”

Designation: Z-283-F  
Codename: Froger  
Variant Type: Tracking / Reacquisition Entity

---

A. IDENTIFICATION

- Facial structure does not clearly align with known deep-sea taxa
    
- Hypothesized mimicry of Antennariidae or Chauliodus species
    
- Visual classification remains inconclusive
    

---

B. BEHAVIORAL PROFILE

Z-283-F demonstrates apparent target-tracking behavior:

- Detects nearby organisms
    
- Temporarily halts movement
    
- Reorients toward origin point of perceived targets
    
- Exhibits behavior consistent with “searching” or “tracking” prey
    

---

C. ENGAGEMENT PATTERN

- After targets take cover, Z-283-F departs briefly
    
- Returns after a short interval to re-engage
    
- Suggests persistent area-based tracking rather than immediate pursuit
    

---

D. OPERATIONAL DIRECTIVE

- Utilize side rooms located outside primary traversal paths
    
- Avoid remaining in open or predictable positions
    
- Do not re-emerge until confirmed departure of entity
    
- Maintain concealment until area is verified clear
    

---

## V. Z-283-C — “CHAINSMOKER”

Designation: Z-283-C  
Codename: Chainsmoker  
Variant Type: Slow-Motion / High-Stability Entity

---

A. IDENTIFICATION

- Morphology resembles decompressed Psychrolutes specimens
    
- Green-hued coloration
    
- Continuous emission of smoke-like material from body
    

---

B. BEHAVIORAL PROFILE

- Slowest recorded variant of Z-283
    
- Movement is deliberate and easily trackable
    
- Lower immediate threat level compared to other variants
    

---

C. OPERATIONAL CHARACTERISTICS

- Extended approach time allows for preemptive response
    
- Reduced acceleration and limited sudden movement capability
    

---

D. OPERATIONAL DIRECTIVE

- Personnel are afforded additional reaction time
    
- Recommended evasive actions:
    

- Seek cover in lockers or reinforced side rooms
    
- Avoid main traversal paths
    
- Maintain awareness until entity passes
    

Note:  
Due to naming inconsistencies in prior field reports, verify designation before issuing response protocols.
