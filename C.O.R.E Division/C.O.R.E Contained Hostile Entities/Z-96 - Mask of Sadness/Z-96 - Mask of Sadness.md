---

II. OVERVIEW

Z-96 is a smiling theater mask that functions as an assimilation trigger. When worn by a biological or non-biological host, the artifact initiates the M.o.S.A. (Mask of Sadness Assimilation) process, converting the host into a sentient biomass entity that expands through the absorption of surrounding organic matter.

The entity is classified as both an anomalous object and a transformational catalyst capable of initiating large-scale bio-organic expansion events.

---

III. HISTORICAL RECORD

Earliest Recorded Incident: September 2nd, 1666

- Location: Theatrical performance venue
    
- Usage: Substitution for a missing comedy mask
    
- Outcome:
    

- Host initiated M.o.S.A. process mid-performance
    
- Assimilation of 710 out of 725 individuals present
    
- Victims included cast, crew, and audience
    

Following initial expansion:

- Z-96 breached containment (pre-modern)
    
- Spread into surrounding urban regions
    
- Chain reaction incident resulted in widespread fire outbreak
    
- Entity became trapped within an uncontrolled inferno
    
- Reduced to residual ash
    

Mask was later recovered and secured within Buckingham Palace vaults. The incident was omitted from official historical records due to limited corroboration and widespread disbelief.

- Placement upon any host (biological or non-biological, including deceased entities)
    

Process:

1. Host contact triggers assimilation sequence
    
2. Host is converted into cohesive biomass
    
3. Resulting entity gains sentience and mobility
    
4. Entity seeks additional biological material for absorption
    

Post-Assimilation Behavior:

- Continuous expansion via ingestion of organic matter
    
- Consolidation into larger biomass structures
    
- Temporary retreat once sufficient mass is accumulated
    
- Internal digestion and structural stabilization
