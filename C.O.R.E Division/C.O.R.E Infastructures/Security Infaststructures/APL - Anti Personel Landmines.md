I. DEVICE DESIGNATION

Device Name: Anti-Personnel Landmine (APL)  
Device Type: Pressure-Triggered Area Denial Mine  
Developed By: C.O.R.E Division

---

II. OVERVIEW

The Anti-Personnel Landmine (APL) is a compact, high-efficiency area denial device engineered for large-scale terrain control and automated deployment. Designed to suppress infantry movement and prevent flanking maneuvers, the APL is optimized for integration with Aperture’s Automated Minelayer Sprayer (AMS) system.

While manual deployment authorization exists, issuance to standard infantry units is limited due to the operational preference for the APD in controlled environments.

---

III. DEPLOYMENT PARAMETERS

- AMS Capacity: Up to 30 APL units per deployment cycle  
      
    
- Effective Use Cases:  
      
    

- Open terrain denial  
      
    
- Perimeter fortification  
      
    
- Flank suppression zones  
      
    

Primary Function:

- Restrict movement across wide areas  
      
    
- Inflict damage upon unauthorized traversal  
      
    
- Create persistent environmental threat fields  
      
    

---

IV. CONSTRUCTION & PAYLOAD

Legacy Model:

- Explosive Core: ~300g gunpowder  
      
    
- Casing Function: Fragmentation shrapnel  
      
    

Current Model (Standard Issue):

- Explosive Core: Gunpowder-based charge  
      
    
- Integrated Component: Programmable Nitrogen Canister  
      
    

---

V. AUTO-DISARM SYSTEM

Each APL includes a timed nitrogen-based neutralization mechanism:

- Default Activation: 1 month post-priming  
      
    
- Adjustable Timer Range: 6 hours – 12 years  
      
    

Function:  
Upon activation, the nitrogen canister releases and freezes the internal explosive compound, rendering the device inert.

Post-Disarm Status:

- Safe for recovery under standard conditions  
      
    
- No specialized protective equipment required  
      
    

Refurbishment Requirements:

- Gunpowder refill  
      
    
- Nitrogen canister replacement  
      
    

---

VI. RECOVERY EFFICIENCY

- Retrieval Mortality Rate: 0% (recorded)  
      
    
- Operational Benefit:  
      
    

- Reduced equipment loss  
      
    
- Increased redeployment efficiency  
      
    
- Lower logistical strain  
      
    

---

VII. IDENTIFICATION SYSTEM

APL units are equipped with LED-based proximity indicators:

- Respond to Aperture-issued identification tags  
      
    
- Emit:  
      
    

- Periodic beep  
      
    
- Brief illumination every 4–6 seconds  
      
    

Purpose:

- Assist friendly personnel in locating deployed mines  
      
    
- Reduce accidental proximity exposure  
      
    

---

VIII. TRIGGER MECHANISM

Trigger Type: Pressure Activation

Critical Limitation:  
The pressure trigger does NOT recognize identification tags.

→ Any applied weight results in immediate detonation, regardless of affiliation.

---

IX. PROTECTIVE COUNTERMEASURES

Aperture-issued combat boots include:

- Reinforced steel structure  
      
    
- Lightweight concrete layering  
      
    

Purpose:

- Mitigate blast force from partial or indirect activation  
      
    
- Reduce severity of long-term injury  
      
    

Note:  
Protection is limited. Direct activation remains highly dangerous.
