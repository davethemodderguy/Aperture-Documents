I. DEVICE DESIGNATION

Device Name: Anti-Personnel Doorstopper (APD)  
Device Type: Tripwire-Activated Area Denial Charge  
Developed By: C.O.R.E Division

---

II. OVERVIEW

The Anti-Personnel Doorstopper (APD) is a compact, deployable tripwire device designed for entry denial, choke point control, and rapid area suppression. Engineered for field operatives within the Infiltration Division, the APD provides a scalable lethality profile while maintaining minimal deployment footprint.

Each unit is optimized for integration into standard “Bunker” kits, enabling efficient transport and rapid installation during operations.

---

III. DEPLOYMENT PARAMETERS

- Standard Carry Capacity: 3 units per operative  
      
    
- Deployment Environment:  
      
    

- Doorways  
      
    
- Corridors  
      
    
- Narrow passageways  
      
    
- Containment access points  
      
    

Primary Function:

- Deny passage  
      
    
- Control movement  
      
    
- Force rerouting or engagement  
      
    

---

IV. CONSTRUCTION & PAYLOAD

Primary Payload:

- 1,200 × 3.4 SS corroded steel ball bearings  
      
    

Secondary Charge:

- 750g auxiliary explosive compound  
      
    

Effect Profile:

- High-velocity fragmentation dispersal  
      
    
- Wide-area kinetic damage depending on selected mode  
      
    

---

V. TARGETING MODES

The APD includes an integrated control unit with selectable detonation profiles:

1. WARNING

- Spread Angle: 30° downward  
      
    
- Target Zone: Feet / lower extremities  
      
    
- Purpose: Minimal injury, deterrence, civilian compliance  
      
    

2. INJURE

- Spread Angle: 15° downward  
      
    
- Target Zone: Knees / femur  
      
    
- Purpose: Mobility disablement  
      
    

3. GUILLOTINE

- Spread Angle: 45° upward  
      
    
- Target Zone: Upper thigh / lower spine  
      
    
- Result: Severe trauma, high probability of dismemberment  
      
    

4. LETHAL

- Spread Angle: 80° upward  
      
    
- Target Zone: Head / upper torso  
      
    
- Result: Immediate fatality  
      
    

5. COLLATERAL

- Spread Pattern: Omnidirectional  
      
    
- Target Zone: All surrounding entities  
      
    
- Result: Maximum area saturation  
      
    

---

VI. IDENTIFICATION & FRIEND-OR-FOE SYSTEM

APD units include LED-based identification indicators that:

- Respond to embedded tags in Aperture-issued uniforms  
      
    
- Assist in post-deployment visibility and recovery  
      
    
- Provide situational awareness to allied personnel  
      
    

Critical Limitation:  
The laser tripwire trigger does NOT recognize identification tags.

→ Any interruption of the beam results in immediate detonation, regardless of affiliation.

---

VII. SAFETY & RISK MITIGATION

While APDs pose a significant risk to all biological targets, Aperture-issued uniforms are equipped with:

- Layered Kevlar plating  
      
    
- Carbon composite reinforcement  
      
    

These materials are designed to:

- Reduce penetration from low-angle dispersal modes  
      
    
- Improve survivability in non-lethal or misaligned detonations  
      
    

Note: Protection is not guaranteed, particularly under “Guillotine,” “Lethal,” or “Collateral” modes.

---

VIII. OPERATIONAL DOCTRINE

- APDs prioritize area control over target discrimination  
      
    
- Friendly risk is considered acceptable within operational parameters  
      
    
- Devices are intended to:  
      
    

- Delay enemy movement  
      
    
- Funnel targets into controlled zones  
      
    
- Supplement IDS and other automated defenses  
      
    

Improper placement or failure to communicate deployment zones is classified as operator negligence.

---

IX. FAILURE & RECOVERY

- Undetonated units must be handled by authorized personnel only  
      
    
- Disarmament procedures are REDACTED  
      
    
- Recovery is facilitated via LED identification system  
      
    

Unauthorized tampering is classified as a Level 4 violation.
