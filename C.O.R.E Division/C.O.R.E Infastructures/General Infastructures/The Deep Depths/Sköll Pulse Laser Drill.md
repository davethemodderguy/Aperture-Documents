**II. OVERVIEW**

The Sköll Pulse Laser Drill is a subterranean excavation system located within the quarry directly beneath the Sky Blacksite. Despite its designation, the system does not utilize conventional laser-based drilling, instead operating through a high-intensity plasma arc capable of extreme thermal excavation.

Originally proposed by **Director Kepler** as an advancement in industrial mining, the project was formally rejected by the Aperture board. Development proceeded covertly between **2015–2017** following unauthorized backing secured through **Director Lucious**.

The system is currently classified as a **high-yield strategic asset**.

---

- Configuration: Circular, bedrock-embedded structure
- Reinforcement: Heavy alloy composite shell

**Stabilization:**

**IV. CORE MECHANISM**

At the center of the Drill is a primary funnel assembly containing multiple **nozzle arrays**.

**Operational Process:**

1. Injection of inert **argon–neon gas mixture**
2. Superheating of gas within chamber
3. Expulsion as a concentrated plasma column

**Thermal Output:**

- Operational Range: **1,000°C – 10,000°C**

**Effect:**

- Rapid melting of geological layers
- Partial to complete vaporization of dense strata

---

**V. ARC STABILIZATION SYSTEM**

Surrounding the plasma column are **Arc Stabiliser Rotors (ASRs)**.

**Functions:**

- Maintain plasma cohesion
- Dynamically adjust arc trajectory
- Monitor real-time stability fluctuations

ASRs operate continuously during active cycles to prevent arc dispersion or structural damage.
