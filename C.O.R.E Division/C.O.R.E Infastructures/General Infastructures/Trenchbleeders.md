I. SYSTEM DESIGNATION

System Name: Trenchbleeder Class Walker  
System Type: Mobile Oil Rig / Autonomous Mining Platform  
Control Authority: Lucy AI

---

II. OVERVIEW

The Trenchbleeder Class Walker is a large-scale autonomous extraction platform designed for resource acquisition within the Let-Vand zone. Functioning as both a mobile oil rig and mining apparatus, each unit is capable of independent operation without onboard personnel.

The platform integrates navigation, scanning, and extraction systems into a self-sustaining industrial unit, enabling continuous operation under dynamic environmental conditions.

---

III. PHYSICAL SPECIFICATIONS

- Mass: 220,600 tonnes
    
- Dimensions:
    

- Height: ~1,500 studs
    
- Width: ~1,000 studs
    

Structural Characteristics:

- Reinforced industrial framework
    
- Buoyant design optimized for low-density aquatic environments
    
- Stability systems engineered to maintain upright posture within trench conditions
    

---

IV. SENSOR & NAVIGATION SYSTEMS

Each Trenchbleeder is equipped with an advanced LiDAR-camera scanning array mounted atop its flare stack.

Capabilities:

- High-resolution environmental mapping
    
- Resource deposit detection
    
- Terrain and obstacle recognition
    
- Real-time navigational guidance
    

These systems allow autonomous traversal and positioning within the Let-Vand zone.

---

V. OPERATIONAL CONTROL

All Trenchbleeder units are operated by Lucy AI, enabling:

- Fully autonomous navigation
    
- Independent decision-making for extraction cycles
    
- Continuous operation without internal crew
    

Human oversight is only required during maintenance or emergency intervention scenarios.

---

VI. OPERATIONAL CYCLE

1. Traverse Let-Vand zone using LiDAR-assisted navigation
    
2. Identify resource-rich deposits (minerals, natural gas, petroleum)
    
3. Position directly above extraction site
    
4. Initiate extraction operations
    
5. Continue until:
    

- Resource depletion, or
    
- Cargo storage capacity is reached
    

7. Return to designated loading dock
    
8. Offload materials and resume scanning operations
    

---

VII. MAINTENANCE REQUIREMENTS

- Scheduled Maintenance Interval: Biweekly
    
- Maintenance Scope:
    

- Structural inspection
    
- Sensor calibration
    
- Extraction system servicing
    
- Power and propulsion diagnostics
    

In the event Lucy AI is offline:

- A skeleton crew of 12 personnel is capable of sustaining basic operational functions
    
- Full performance is restored upon AI reintegration
    

---

VIII. ENVIRONMENTAL CONSTRAINTS

Trenchbleeders are specifically engineered for operation within low-density aquatic environments.

Critical Limitation:

- Units are unable to operate on dry land
    
- Exiting Let-Vand zone results in:
    

- Sudden pressure differential
    
- Immediate hull implosion
    

Recovery from such an event requires extensive reconstruction and operational suspension.

---

IX. OPERATIONAL RESILIENCE

Trenchbleeders are designed to remain functional during:

- Blacksite construction
    
- Maintenance cycles
    
- Lockdowns
    
- Site-wide emergencies
    

Their systems maintain operational autonomy under disrupted facility conditions.
