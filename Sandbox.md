Z-90, Codename: "Wall Dwellers", is a large arthropod-like species defined by their flat front legs, which extend from head to toe and possess a pale, chalky surface. It is the sole known member of the newly discovered genus ████████████████.

  
The first recorded encounter occurred on July 20th, 2023, within the abandoned ██████ Office Complex in Los Angeles. Four social media influencers entered the building following reports of disappearances and rumours of it being haunted. When they failed to appear at a brand event in Beverly Hills later that night, concerned connections used GPS tracking to locate their last known position and alerted local law enforcement.

  
Two responding LAPD officers discovered multiple rectangular perforations in the office's interior walls and immediately called for backup. Additional responders reported the rpesence of an unidentified "moving wall" on the premises. Due to the encounter's anomalous nature, the LAPD called in Urbanshade to take over the investigation. Upon arrival, the first task force, Squadron Echo, unexpectedly encountered not just a single instance of Z-90 but an entire colony inhabiting the structure.

Backup Infiltration officers were deployed on the scene shortly after a distress call to recover the bodies and belongings of both the influencers and Squadron Echo. Over 110 Z-90 specimens were captured using tungsten-mesh nets and transferred to the Blacksite for further study. The initial witnesses on site were provided a "generous donation" in exchange for their silence and cooperation. They were instructed to release a statement attributing the deaths of the four individuals to a construction accident.

  
Operatives recovered personal items belonging to the missing individuals, including a handheld camera containing video footage depicting multiple Z-90 forms pursuing the missing individuals and ultimately consuming them. Research indicates that the anatomy of Z-90 differs significantly from that of documented animal life. Specimens lack most vital mammalian organ systems, possessing only an integumentary and a digestive system, making them more biologically akin to a carnivorous plant. Z-90 possesses two large raptoral forelimbs, which they use to camouflage themselves and to form a trapdoor-like concealment mechanism when in a dormant state. These forelimbs measure approximately 7.4 studs in height and cover the entire body, hiding a Z-90 specimen's torso completely. This adaptation allows Z-90s to embed within and seamlessly visually merge with surfaces. The physiology of Z-90 has prompted several questions and hypotheses, including how it hunts or exhibits spatial awareness in the absence of auditory or sensory organs. Several living specimens were transferred to observation chambers where researchers could observe their biological mechanisms and camouflage properties under controlled conditions.

In their dormant state, Z-90's flat plates appear structurally identical to the surrounding material. However, microscopic analysis of samples scraped from these surfaces reveals an integumentary tissue composition with unique intercelluar structures. Arrays of microscopic "gear" cells cover Z-90's large anterior tibial camouflage plates. These cells are lined with serrated edges and are porous, containing internal digestive organelles that produce and secrete specialised digestive enzymes, which are mixed with stomach acid. Z-90's digestive enzymes can modify their active sites to accommodate almost any molecule, allowing them and stomach acid to corrode even the toughest materials.

  
When embedding in a surface, they push these plates into the material and perform subtle circular motions; the "teeth" of these cells interlock with one another, transmitting rotational motion and forming billions of small "chainsaws." The sawing motion produces multiple surface cracks, and the pressure exerted as it pushes into the wall forces the acid through the cells' pores, melting the small particles. This process allows Z-90 to create and embed within its burrow in a matter of minutes.

  
The cells lining Z-90's plates have been termed "torque cells." Their teethed edges, when not used to saw into material, fold into the acid pores to complete Z-90's camouflaging process. The degree of folding depends on the material's texture; the teeth fold fully around smooth surfaces and only partially around rougher ones. These torque cells are translucent, allowing the layers of chromatophores, iridophores, and leucophores beneath to adapt to the colour and reflectance of the surrounding material. This structure is similar to that of species such as cuttlefish, which has prompted theories about their phylogenetic similarities.

  
Once embedded, Z-90 remains dormant until it detects potential food in the area. Z-90 functions as an ambush predator and will emerge from the burrow in the wall or floor, stalking its prey after they have left the immediate area. Despite their large stature, they remain almost entirely silent whilst in pursuit, as their legs are primarily used for balance rather than movement. If they are observed by their victim while stalking, Z-90 will attempt to conceal itself behind their forelimbs and remain stationary. They will only retreat after an extended period once detected. During tests, they have been observed feigning a retreat to lull targets into a false sense of security before returning to initiate a secondary ambush.

  
Z-90 also appears to detect when it is being perceived through walls and will pause entirely; this phenomenon remains unexplained despite numerous trials. Once caught, the prey is immobilised behind its large forelimbs, before being shovelled into its mouth and sprays with acid glands near the back of the throat and decapitated, making death instantaneous. If its target is alone, the Z-90 instance immediately begins consumption, ingesting it in its entirety. However, if its target is in a group, Z-90 prioritises isolating it from its peers to achieve a higher success rate.

  
The first observations of Z-90's flesh properties were made after an intern's sick Deep Sea Bunny entered a specimen morgue room containing a deceased Z-90 specimen and consumed a portion of its foreleg tissue. Contrary to expectations that the DSB would deteriorate, the animal remained fine and returned to a healthy state within hours. This prompted initial animal experimentation; a group of 15 sick or injured Deep Sea Bunnies was fed chunks of Z-90 flesh, alongside 5 controls. All but one in the experimental group recovered fully within 24 hours. Blood tests showed significantly healthier blood and immune cells compared to controls.

  
Spectroscopic analysis identified a new vitamin within Z-90s' flesh, with a composition similar to but distinct from and significantly more potent than Vitamin E. This vitamin was named Vitamin E90, in correspondence to its functional similarity to Vitamin E and 90 to represent its Z-class. LR-P trials yielded results similar to those of the Deep Sea Bunny trials, and the Hazards & Ethics Division eventually approved Z-90 flesh for consumption. Z-90 flesh has since become a beloved ingredient on the Blacksite's cafeteria menu, under the name "Hadal Crab" for its flavour similar to crab and calamari. It is used in salads, burgers, nacho dips, and various other dishes to promote employee health.

  
Research on the further utilisation of Z-90's characteristics is ongoing. Proposals for the valorisation of Z-90 include replicating its camouflage for use in tactical uniforms and military equipment, adapting its restorative properties for medical applications, and using its digestive acid for waste destruction. Once these properties can be effectively duplicated, Urbanshade will conceal and duplicate all remaining specimens solely for culinary purposes.

Research indicates that the anatomy of Z-90 differs significantly from previously documented humanoid entities or any known animals. They lack most vital mammalian organ systems, containing only an integumentary and digestive system. When undisguised, their frontal shell takes on the appearance of calcium sulfate dihydrate, more commonly known as “drywall.” However, microscopic analysis of shell samples reveals that the surface is more akin to integument tissues. Z-90 epidermal cells possess remarkable camouflage potential, as experiments show these cells are capable of changing the skin’s colour, texture, and material composition to mimic its architectural surroundings seamlessly.

Z-90 instances possess specialized digestive enzymes that can modify their active site to accommodate almost any molecule and facilitate hydrogenation in combination with their stomach acid. They excrete this enzyme-acid solution through numerous holes scattered around the rear legs, head, and torso, enabling the rapid corrosion of even the toughest materials. It is said to be nearly equivalent to agents such as fluoroantimonic acid due to the large number of free hydrogen cations and rapid catalysis facilitated by the enzymes, allowing Z-90 to melt and embed itself into walls within minutes. Despite the presence of this potent concoction in their system, the flesh of Z-90 is viable for consumption, possessing regenerative properties which are currently under study.

Once embedded, Z-90 remains dormant until potential prey is nearby. It emerges from the wall, follows its target, disables them by kicking the back of their knee, then severs their neck. If its prey turns around before being attacked, Z-90 will retreat upon being spotted. Some instances may feign a retreat to lull targets into a false sense of security before returning to ambush their victims. If its target is alone, the Z-90 instance immediately begins consumption, ingesting it in its entirety. However, if its target is in a group, Z-90 prioritises isolating its target from its peers for a higher success rate.

Research is still ongoing regarding the characteristics of Z-90. Proposals for the valorization of Z-90 involve replicating its camouflage for use in tactical uniforms and military equipment. Another proposal focuses on adapting the restorative properties found in Z-90’s tissue for medical applications. Once these properties can be effectively duplicated, Urbanshade will terminate all instances of Z-90 in containment.
